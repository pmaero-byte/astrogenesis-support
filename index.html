Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are the calculations?

AstroGenesis uses the Swiss Ephemeris (the same engine used by professional astrologers worldwide) for planetary positions, accurate to arcsecond precision. Each of our 12 calculation engines applies tradition-specific rules for dignity, aspects, and interpretation.

Do I need to know my exact birth time?

For the most accurate chart, an exact birth time is recommended. Without it, the ascendant, house placements, and certain divisional charts (Navamsa, Dasamsa) may be inaccurate. If you don't know your birth time, you can still get planet-in-sign interpretations.

What makes AstroGenesis different from other astrology apps?

AstroGenesis is the only app that runs 12 independent astrological calculation engines simultaneously, then compares their findings in a Council analysis. No other app offers interpretations from Parashara, Ptolemy, Vettius Valens, Ibn Ezra, Bonatti, and 7 other historical astrologers — each speaking in their own scholarly voice.

Is my data private?

Yes. AstroGenesis does not collect your name, email, or any personal identifiers. Your birth profiles are stored locally on your device. We do not use analytics, ads, or third-party tracking. See our Privacy Policy for details.

What is the Council?

The Council is AstroGenesis's unique consensus engine. It runs your chart through all 12 astrologers, then identifies where they agree (consensus), where they disagree (divergence), and unique discoveries from each tradition. This multi-perspective analysis is not available in any other app.

What are divisional charts (D9, D10)?

Divisional charts (vargas) are a Vedic technique that subdivides the zodiac to reveal specific life areas. The Navamsa (D9) reveals soul-level patterns and marriage, while the Dasamsa (D10) focuses on career. AstroGenesis provides interpretations for these from both Vedic and cross-tradition perspectives.

The app is slow to load / Council takes time

AstroGenesis runs 12 independent calculation engines in real-time. The Council analysis may take 3-5 seconds as each engine computes your chart independently before the consensus algorithm compares results. This is normal — you are receiving 12 analyses simultaneously.
